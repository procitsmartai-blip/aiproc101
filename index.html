Definition — For Dummies

AI in plain English

AI is a computer system that can learn from experience, understand your language, and make decisions — the way a really smart assistant would.

It's not magic. It's not sentient. It's pattern recognition at scale — trained on more text, data, and examples than any human could read in a lifetime.

Think of it as
A very well-read intern who never sleeps
It can
Read, write, summarise, search, code, plan
It cannot
Replace your judgment, relationships, or context
Types of AI

What's out there — and what's next

ML
Machine Learning
Learns from data to spot patterns and make predictions. The engine behind spam filters, recommendations, and fraud detection.
eg. Netflix recommendations · spam filter · bank fraud alerts
Gen AI
Generative AI
Creates new content — text, images, code, presentations. The AI you interact with by typing a question or request.
eg. ChatGPT · Claude · Midjourney · Gemini
SaaS AI
AI in Tools You Use
AI baked into apps you already have. You might already be using it — autocomplete, smart search, email suggestions, smart replies.
eg. Copilot in Excel · Grammarly · Notion AI
AGI
Artificial General Intelligence
The dream: AI that can do anything a human can across any domain. Not here yet. But actively being researched.
Status: active research · no confirmed timeline

Work Use · The Golden Rule

Better context = better answers

The more you tell AI about your situation, the more useful it becomes. Use SPECS.

S
Situation
Give context about your role & company
P
Purpose
What are you trying to achieve?
E
Example
Show what a good answer looks like
C
Constraints
Budget, timeline, format limits
S
Style
How formal or detailed should the response be?
Example prompt using SPECS
"I'm a procurement officer at a manufacturing company [S].
I need to evaluate 3 suppliers for packaging materials [P].
Here's an example comparison I've done before: [...] [E].
Budget under 50K USD, deadline end of month [C].
Please make it a simple table, not too formal [S]."

💡 Remember

Don't just say "compare suppliers." Give it your context. The AI doesn't know your company, your constraints, or your standards — unless you tell it.

How to Use AI · Reality Check

It's not all gold.

AI is powerful — but it makes mistakes, can be manipulated, and doesn't know what it doesn't know. These are real cases.

⚖️
The Lawyer Who Got Sanctioned
Mata v. Avianca · USA · 2023

A lawyer submitted a court filing with 6 precedent cases found by ChatGPT. Every case was made up. None existed. The judge sanctioned the lawyer and the firm.

Lesson · AI sounds confident even when completely wrong. Always verify facts, names, and numbers independently.
🔐
Samsung's Confidential Data Leak
Samsung · South Korea · 2023

Engineers pasted proprietary source code and internal meeting notes into ChatGPT for help. That data potentially became part of OpenAI's training set — outside Samsung's control forever.

Lesson · Never paste confidential pricing, supplier contracts, or negotiation data into a public AI tool.
✈️
Air Canada's Chatbot Liability
Air Canada · Canada · 2024

Air Canada's chatbot gave a passenger wrong refund information. Air Canada said "that was the bot, not us." Court disagreed — they had to honour what the bot promised.

Lesson · Whatever your AI says to stakeholders = your company said it. Always review before sending.
🚗
The $1 Chevy Tahoe
Chevrolet Dealership · USA · 2023

A customer prompted a dealership chatbot with clever instructions until it agreed — in writing — to sell a brand new Chevy Tahoe for $1. The screenshot went viral overnight.

Lesson · AI can be manipulated. Never give it authority over final decisions — keep humans in the loop.
